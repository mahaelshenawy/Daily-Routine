🌅 Morning Routine: Starting the Day

Vocabulary Definition Example
Wake up To stop sleeping "I wake up at 6:30 AM."
Get up To get out of bed "I get up at 7:00 AM."
Brush teeth To clean your teeth "I brush my teeth twice."
Wash face To clean your face "I wash my face first."

🌅 Morning Routine: Getting Ready

Vocabulary Definition Example
Have breakfast To eat your first meal "I have breakfast at 7:30."
Get dressed To put on your clothes "I get dressed for work."
Brush hair To tidy your hair "I brush my hair quickly."
Leave house To go out for work "I leave the house at 8:00."

☀️ Afternoon Routine: The Middle of the Day

Vocabulary Definition Example
Have lunch To eat the midday meal "I have lunch at 1:00 PM."
Work / Study To do your main activity "I study for three hours."
Take a break To rest for a short time "I take a break at 3:30 PM."
Commute To travel to/from work "My commute takes 40 mins."

🌆 Evening Routine: Winding Down

Vocabulary Definition Example
Go home To return to your house "I go home at 5:30 PM."
Cook dinner To prepare the meal "I cook dinner for my family."
Have dinner To eat the evening meal "We have dinner together."
Relax To rest and enjoy "I relax and watch TV."

🌙 Night Routine: Preparing for Sleep

Vocabulary Definition Example
Have a shower To wash your body "I have a shower at night."
Read a book A way to relax "I read a book in bed."
Set the alarm To prepare for morning "I set the alarm for 6 AM."
Go to bed To go to sleep "I go to bed at 11 PM."

⏰ Telling the Time with Routines

Use "at"

  • Specific times: at 7:00
  • Midday: at noon
  • Midnight: at midnight
  • Night: at night

Use "in the"

  • Morning: in the morning
  • Afternoon: in the afternoon
  • Evening: in the evening

Example: "I wake up at 7:00 in the morning."

📖 Grammar: Present Simple (I, You, We, They)

The Rule: Use the base form of the verb for habits and routines. Do not add "s" at the end of the verb for these subjects.

I wake up

You have lunch

We go home

They watch TV

Examples:

"I brush my teeth every morning."

"They leave the house at 8:00."

📖 Grammar: Present Simple (He, She, It)

The Rule: For He, She, and It, we must add -s or -es to the end of the base verb.

He wakes up at 7:00.

She has lunch at noon.

It takes 30 minutes.

He goes home early.

💡 Tip: Remember the "s" for the third person singular!

📍 Sequencing Words: Telling a Story

First

To start the sequence

Then / Next

For following actions

After that

To continue the flow

Finally

To end the sequence

Example Story:

"First, I wake up at 6:30. Then, I have a quick breakfast. After that, I brush my teeth and get dressed. Finally, I leave for work at 8:00."

💡 Tip: Use these to show your habits and consistency!

📍 Word Order: Where Do They Go?

Standard Rule

Subject + Adverb + Verb

"I always drink coffee in the morning."

"She never goes to bed late."

"They usually have lunch at 1:00."

The Exception

Verb "to be"

The adverb goes after the verb "to be" (am, is, are).

"I am always happy."

"He is often busy."

🔗 Phrasal Verbs for Routines

Phrasal Verb Meaning Example
Wake up Stop sleeping "I wake up at 6 AM."
Get up Get out of bed "I get up at 6:15 AM."
Tidy up Make a room neat "I tidy up my desk."
Wash up Clean the dishes "I wash up after dinner."
Catch up Talk about news "We catch up at lunch."

⚠️ Common Mistakes to Avoid

Third Person Singular

❌ He wake up early.

✅ He wakes up early.

Adverb Word Order

❌ I have usually lunch.

✅ I usually have lunch.

Prepositions

❌ In the night.

✅ At night.

Vocabulary Confusion

Wake up: Your eyes open, you stop sleeping.

Get up: You physically leave your bed.

💡 Quick Tip: Always check your verb endings and prepositions when describing your daily habits!
